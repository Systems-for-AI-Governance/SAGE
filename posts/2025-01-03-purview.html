Microsoft Purview and SAGE: Bridging the Gap in AI Governance and Compliance

Microsoft Purview is a comprehensive data governance solution designed to secure, manage, and govern data across hybrid environments. Its primary focus is on data security, with features targeting risks like data exfiltration and compliance monitoring in generative AI applications.

Understanding Microsoft Purview

  • Data Map: Automatic discovery and scanning of data sources for inventory creation.
  • Data Catalog: Centralized repository with metadata and search capabilities.
  • Data Classification: Automatic detection of sensitive data types such as PII and financial information.
  • Policy Management: Centralized definition and enforcement of governance policies.
  • Data Lineage: Visualization of data flows and transformations.
  • Access Controls: Role-based governance of data assets.

Strengths and Limitations

Strengths

  • Strong focus on preventing data leaks and exfiltration in generative AI applications.
  • Features like the AI Hub monitor user interactions with AI systems and recommend security actions.
  • Compliance features highlight unfulfilled controls, providing security recommendations.

Limitations

  • Limited Scope in Healthcare AI Compliance: Does not address specialized regulations like SaMD and ONC's HTI-1.
  • Workflow Gaps: Users still require expertise to navigate workflows, as Purview does not provide end-to-end guidance.
  • Narrow Focus: Primarily focuses on data security rather than evaluating product performance, managing lifecycle, or addressing biases.
  • Out-of-Scope Controls: Some compliance and security controls are limited to Microsoft platform implementations.
  • Experimental Features: Tools like the AI Hub are still evolving and lack comprehensive functionality.

SAGE: Closing the Gaps in AI Governance and Compliance

As AI adoption accelerates in healthcare, there is a growing need for governance tools that address the unique challenges posed by AI systems. SAGE is an open-source platform specifically designed to integrate with tools like Microsoft Purview by addressing its gaps.

Challenges in Healthcare AI

  • Complex Technology: AI outputs are stochastic, not deterministic, requiring specialized oversight.
  • Regulatory Compliance: New regulations like SaMD and HTI-1 impose stringent requirements and penalties for non-compliance.
  • Liability Risks: Deployment risks include patient safety, bias, and security vulnerabilities.
  • Evolving Standards: Fast-paced advancements necessitate adaptive governance tools.

Features and Capabilities of SAGE

SAGE's capabilities are designed to empower organizations in managing AI systems comprehensively, ensuring compliance, safety, and operational efficiency. Below are the key features and their tangible benefits:

Governance:

  • Prioritization of AI Projects: Aligns projects with organizational goals, ensuring resources are focused on impactful initiatives.
  • Risk Identification: Surfaces potential risks early in the lifecycle, allowing proactive management.
  • Regulatory Compliance: Provides alignment with evolving legal frameworks, reducing non-compliance penalties.
  • Problem Analysis: Helps identify, prioritize, and define problems comprehensively, enabling targeted solutions.

Risk Management:

  • Proactive Risk Mitigation: Tracks risks like data drift and liability exposure, minimizing disruptions.
  • Technical Suitability Assessment: Evaluates the appropriateness of technical approaches, ensuring feasibility and alignment with objectives.
  • Downstream Impact Analysis: Identifies potential consequences of AI deployments, supporting informed decision-making.
  • External and Internal AI Evaluation: Assesses the viability of building versus buying AI solutions, while evaluating external options for quality and fit.
  • Legal Risk Management: Provides tools to assess and mitigate legal liabilities tied to AI use.

Compliance:

  • Audit Readiness: Maintains a detailed paper trail to demonstrate adherence to regulations like ISO 42001 and HTI-1.
  • Performance Target Definition: Establishes clear metrics to simplify governance, deployment, and decommissioning.
  • Goal Clarity: Prevents feature creep by defining objectives upfront, keeping projects focused and efficient.

Collaboration:

  • Stakeholder Empowerment: Involves clinical, administrative, and executive teams in AI development, fostering cross-functional alignment.
  • Workflow Design and Validation: Develops and tests clinician workflows to ensure smooth integration and usability.
  • Information Dissemination: Automatically generates model fact labels and user manuals for transparent communication.

Operational and Lifecycle Management:

  • Adaptation to Changes: Manages work environment adjustments and monitors AI performance to ensure seamless integration.
  • Auditing Capabilities: Audits AI solutions and their environments to sustain long-term effectiveness.
  • Lifecycle Decisions: Determines when updates, decommissioning, or adaptations are necessary, minimizing disruptions.
  • Expansion Feasibility: Evaluates suitability for new settings, ensuring scalability and consistency.
  • End-User Communication: Disseminates updates effectively to end-users, maintaining clarity and trust.

How SAGE Bridges the Gaps in Microsoft Purview

While Microsoft Purview provides strong data security and compliance monitoring, SAGE extends its capabilities in several critical areas to offer a comprehensive approach to AI governance and compliance:

Filling Compliance Gaps:

  • Addresses specialized regulations like SaMD, ONC's HTI-1 Rule, and the HHS-OIG Information Blocking Rule.
  • Tracks compliance with standards like the NIST AI RMF and FDA guidelines.
  • Manages risks unique to healthcare AI, such as patient safety and liability concerns.

Enhancing Workflow and Collaboration:

  • Provides structured workflows, eliminating the need for domain expertise.
  • Walks users through the entire lifecycle, from evaluation to implementation.
  • Facilitates collaboration across diverse stakeholders, ensuring transparency and accountability.
  • Guides users through end-to-end workflows, minimizing expertise dependency.

Performance and Bias Management:

  • Tracks whether AI solutions meet performance targets.
  • Monitors and mitigates biases to ensure fairness and reliability.

Lifecycle Management and Risk Mitigation:

  • Enables evaluation and comparison of multiple AI products.
  • Ensures continuous monitoring and optimization throughout the AI lifecycle.
  • Provides real-time updates on incidents, risks, and ROI.
  • Surfaces risks early and often to prevent issues from escalating.
  • Determines when updates, decommissioning, or adaptations are necessary, minimizing disruptions.
  • Evaluates suitability for new settings, ensuring scalability and consistency.

Comprehensive Governance:

  • Goes beyond identifying unfulfilled controls by actively helping organizations implement and monitor them.
  • Strengthens transparency and accountability through robust auditing and monitoring mechanisms.
