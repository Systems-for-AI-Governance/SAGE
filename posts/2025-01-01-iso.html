How SAGE helps you comply with ISO 42001

ISO 42001 is a global standard offering guidelines to ensure ethical, secure, and effective AI deployment, with a special focus on high-stakes fields like healthcare where risk management, transparency, and accountability are paramount. It emphasizes risk management, transparency, and accountability, making it a crucial benchmark for governing AI systems in this domain. SAGE actively supports organizations in meeting the objectives of ISO 42001 by ensuring ethical, secure, and effective AI deployment through the following approaches:

Aligning AI Initiatives with Strategic Goals

  • Defines and documents AI-specific policies tailored to organizational objectives, incorporating ethical guidelines, risk tolerance, and compliance mandates.
  • Ensures alignment by integrating AI policies into broader organizational frameworks, such as IT governance and data management strategies.

Facilitating Cross-Functional Collaboration

  • Assigns responsibilities by appointing a dedicated AI governance team with defined roles for decision-making, implementation, and oversight.
  • Establishes structured reporting processes for AI-related incidents, escalations, and performance monitoring.
  • Ensures a holistic governance approach through cross-functional teams that include technical, legal, and operational experts.
  • Embeds AI management seamlessly into existing risk management frameworks.

Ensuring Adequate Infrastructure for AI Systems

  • Identifies and catalogs resources necessary for AI system development and management, including technical, human, and data assets.
  • Supports expertise development through AI-specific training programs.
  • Provides robust technical infrastructure, including secure data storage, reliable tools, and adequate computing power.

Assessing Impacts of AI Systems

  • Uses advanced tools to evaluate social, ethical, and privacy implications of AI systems.
  • Ensures privacy and security through data protection impact assessments (DPIAs) and robust encryption measures.
  • Implements bias and fairness protocols.

Managing the AI System Lifecycle

  • Defines comprehensive policies for each stage of the AI system lifecycle, including design, deployment, monitoring, and decommissioning.
  • Evaluates risks at each stage using formal risk management frameworks like ISO 31000.
  • Facilitates change management through established update protocols.
  • Ensures smooth decommissioning with minimal disruptions.
  • Tests AI systems for accuracy, fairness, and robustness before deployment.
  • Maintains detailed documentation for all lifecycle activities.
  • Manages incidents with robust detection, logging, and response systems.
  • Provides real-time monitoring with dashboards and automated alerts.

Ensuring Data Integrity

  • Enhances data quality through validation tools to ensure accuracy and completeness.
  • Implements privacy-preserving techniques like differential privacy to safeguard sensitive information.
  • Documents data provenance for traceability.
  • Regularly evaluates datasets for bias and establishes processes for continuous refinement and updates.

Promoting Transparency Throughout the AI System Lifecycle

  • Generates explainability reports detailing AI decision-making processes.
  • Creates model fact cards based on 31 attributes defined by the ONC's HTI-1 rule for evaluation, deployment, and maintenance.
  • Develops user training manuals to help end-users effectively utilize AI systems.
  • Communicates potential risks to end-users with clear and concise documentation.
  • Fosters stakeholder engagement by enabling comments, discussions, and votes on strategies, risks, goals, and expectations.

Ensuring Appropriate Use of AI Systems

  • Monitors AI usage with audit mechanisms that track adherence to defined boundaries and conditions.
  • Identifies and mitigates unintended outcomes through structured evaluation processes.
  • Prevents misuse by incorporating safeguards such as user training programs and misuse detection algorithms.

Managing Third-Party Audits and Vendor Relationships

  • Ensures third-party compliance with AI standards through detailed due diligence checklists.
  • Establishes accountability for outsourced AI operations.
  • Incorporates AI-specific clauses in contracts to address risks and compliance requirements.
  • Mandates regular third-party audits to maintain alignment with organizational goals.
  • Maintains open communication channels with external partners to address emerging challenges effectively.

By implementing these structured and comprehensive practices, SAGE equips organizations to navigate the complexities of AI governance while ensuring ethical, secure, and effective AI deployment.
