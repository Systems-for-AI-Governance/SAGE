How SAGE helps you comply with the FDA's SaMD guidelines

Non-compliance with FDA regulations for Software as a Medical Device (SaMD), including AI-based applications, can result in severe penalties, including:

  • Product Recall: Mandatory removal of non-compliant software from the market.
  • Fines: Financial penalties based on the severity and impact of the violation.
  • Warning Letters: Official FDA notices that damage reputation and require immediate corrective actions.
  • Injunctions: Court orders halting the distribution or development of the software.
  • Criminal Prosecution: In cases of intentional or egregious violations, individuals or companies may face criminal charges.

Compliance ensures patient safety, market access, and credibility. SAGE helps organizations comply with FDA guidelines when developing, modifying, and deploying internally-developed CDS (Clinical Decision Support) software determined to be devices according to FDA guidelines. How SAGE Supports Compliance

1. Risk-Based Framework

FDA Guideline: SaMD is categorized based on the risk to patients if it malfunctions, using IMDRF's framework:

  • Significance of Information: Drives or informs clinical management decisions.
  • Healthcare Condition: Critical, serious, or non-serious conditions.

SAGE Controls:

  • Risk Assessment Tools: Use ISO 14971 to evaluate and document hazards.
  • Software Categorization: Classify software by risk (e.g., Class II or III for higher risk requiring FDA approval).
  • Failure Mode Analysis: Identify and mitigate potential failure impacts.

2. Software Life Cycle

FDA Guideline: Robust development and maintenance practices are required to ensure software performance throughout its lifecycle.

SAGE Controls:

  • Development Standards: Follow IEC 62304 for design, development, and testing.
  • Validation: Establish automated and manual validation processes.
  • Configuration Management: Track changes to code, requirements, and documentation.
  • Change Control: Document and assess updates or bug fixes.

3. Clinical Evaluation

FDA Guideline: SaMD must demonstrate performance through analytical and clinical validation.

  • Analytical Validation: Ensures technical performance as specified.
  • Clinical Validation: Confirms accurate and beneficial results for the intended use.

SAGE Controls:

  • Test Data: Evaluate datasets for validation and assess for bias.
  • Performance Metrics: Establish measures like sensitivity, specificity, accuracy, and precision.
  • Regulatory Submission: Compile results for FDA 510(k) or PMA submissions.

4. Transparency and Explainability

FDA Guideline: Complex algorithms, including AI, must be interpretable to ensure trust and proper usage.

SAGE Controls:

  • Explainability Methods: Use interpretable AI models and visual explanations for outputs.
  • User Training: Provide materials to explain limitations and proper usage.
  • Labeling Requirements: Include user manuals, warnings, and intended use details.

5. Cybersecurity

FDA Guideline: SaMD must ensure data protection and operational integrity against cyber threats.

SAGE Controls:

  • Encryption Checking: Verify data encryption in transit and at rest using DPIAs.
  • Access Controls: Ensure role-based access and multi-factor authentication.
  • Security Standards: Use NIST Cybersecurity Framework and FDA cybersecurity guidance.
  • Penetration Testing: Schedule regular testing for vulnerabilities and remediate them.

6. Post-Market Surveillance

FDA Guideline: Continuous monitoring is required to maintain safety and effectiveness after launch.

SAGE Controls:

  • Real-Time Monitoring: Detect and report errors or malfunctions promptly.
  • Adverse Event Reporting: Utilize FDA's MAUDE database for issue tracking.
  • Feedback Loops: Gather user feedback to improve usability and safety.
  • Periodic Updates: Release patches for vulnerabilities and performance improvements.

7. Change Management

FDA Guideline: AI/ML-based SaMD requires transparency, real-world performance monitoring, and periodic reporting on updates to the FDA. Changes made to approved devices need to be tested, documented, and monitored.

SAGE Controls:

  • Data Management: Implement collection protocols and quality assurance measures for new training and test data.
  • Determine reference standards and ensure auditing and sequestration of training and test sets.
  • Re-Training: Define objectives and document changes related to machine learning methods and data pre-processing.
  • Establish criteria to initiate performance evaluation.
  • Performance Evaluation: Define assessment metrics, statistical analysis plans, and performance targets.
  • Schedule evaluations based on frequency and triggers.
  • Use "clinicians in the loop" for testing when necessary.
  • Update Procedures: Verify and validate software updates.
  • Plan for global and local updates with clear timelines.
  • Communicate changes transparently to users and stakeholders.
