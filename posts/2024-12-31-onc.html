How SAGE helps you comply with ONC HTI-1 Final Rule

Key Features of the HTI-1 Final Rule

  • Revised CDS Certification Criteria: The rule builds on existing capabilities, streamlining requirements while maintaining conditional criteria for predictive DSIs.
  • Predictive DSI Scope: Narrows the affected DSIs to those supplied by certified health IT developers as part of their Health IT Module.
  • Risk Management Practices: Requires comprehensive risk management for predictive DSIs, including risk analysis, mitigation, and governance.
  • Assurances Maintenance: Introduces ongoing review and update requirements for certification.
  • Transparency and Trust: Calls for clarity in the design, training, and governance of predictive DSIs to improve their safety, fairness, and effectiveness.
  • Health Equity by Design: Mandates addressing bias and health disparities to promote equitable outcomes for all patients.

How SAGE Aligns with HTI-1 Requirements

SAGE offers an integrated solution to ensure compliance with the HTI-1 Final Rule by providing the following capabilities:

Health Equity Integration

SAGE includes an actionable toolkit that helps healthcare providers and IT developers embed health equity principles into their workflows. This ensures predictive DSIs are designed to minimize bias and promote equitable outcomes.

Certification Support

The platform enables evaluation and implementation of applications in line with the DSI certification criteria. Users can manage source attribute information for evidence-based and predictive DSIs, including recording, accessing, and updating these attributes to ensure compliance.

Intervention Risk Management

SAGE facilitates comprehensive risk management practices as defined in the HTI-1 Final Rule. This includes tools to estimate risks, mitigate potential impacts, and document governance practices, all while reducing administrative burdens.

IRM Practices

The platform provides structured support for:

  • Risk Analysis: Estimating the likelihood and magnitude of risks, identifying affected stakeholders, and evaluating sources of risk.
  • Risk Mitigation: Prioritizing and addressing risks, establishing control plans, and validating performance.
  • Governance: Defining roles and responsibilities, managing data as a strategic asset, and enforcing clear communication about DSI limitations and assumptions.

Compliance Infrastructure

SAGE helps organizations establish infrastructure to meet current and upcoming ONC-ACB requirements, including implementing model cards for transparency and accountability in predictive DSIs.

Advancing Transparency and Trustworthiness

SAGE promotes transparency by providing tools for:

  • Reviewing and documenting how predictive DSIs are designed, developed, and validated.
  • Establishing clear governance frameworks to ensure alignment with organizational priorities and regulatory standards.

Supporting an Information Ecosystem

The platform fosters an environment where healthcare organizations can:

  • Evaluate the fairness, validity, and effectiveness of predictive DSIs (FAVES criteria).
  • Encourage the responsible adoption of predictive DSIs to enhance patient care while minimizing risks.

Conclusion

SAGE stands out as a critical tool for healthcare providers and IT developers navigating the complex regulatory landscape introduced by the ONC HTI-1 Final Rule. By addressing key requirements and advancing policy goals such as transparency, trustworthiness, and health equity, SAGE ensures that predictive DSIs are implemented safely, effectively, and equitably.
